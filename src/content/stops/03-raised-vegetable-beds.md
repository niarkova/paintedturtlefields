With the barn and solar panels nearby, this area felt very rectilinear. I designed the vegetable garden with a circular layout to soften those straight lines and make the garden feel more connected to movement, seasons, and the surrounding landscape. Shane built the stone raised beds so they could follow the curves of the design and work with the slope of the land. The four raised beds represent the four seasons and crop rotation. The center bed is reserved for flowers that attract pollinators — I use it to experiment with new annual flowers and a new colour palette each year. Flowers that reseeded get moved to the adjacent cut flower row. Around the vegetable garden, I planted different berries to create a natural fence. Our 2 large dogs Chaos and Mayhem, plus a fox den nearby, also do a good job of deterring pests.
